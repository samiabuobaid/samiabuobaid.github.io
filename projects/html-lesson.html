Meta Data Content

Meta Data is content that sets up the presentation or behavior of the rest of the content, or that sets up the relationship of the document with other documents, or that conveys other information. "base, kink, meta, noscript, script, style, template, title"


Flow Content

Most elemenets that are used in the body of documents and applications are characterized as flow content. This can include: " a, h1, h2, h3, p, div, header, and much more." It seems that many other types of content fll under Flow Content.


Heading Content

Heading content is used to define the header of a document. This includes "h1, h2, h3, h4, h5,and h6."


Phrasing Content

Phrasing content is the text of the document, as well as elements that mark up that text at the intra-paragraph level. A combination of phrasing content is what is used to create paragraphs. This includes "a,link, br, code, and many others". www.w3.org seems to state that elements that a majority of elements hat are categorized as phrasing content can only ontain elements that are also categorized as phrasing content, and not just any flow content.


Embedded Content

Embedded content either imports another resource into the document or content from another vocabulary that is inserted into the document This includes "audio, canvas, embed, iframe, img, math, object, picture, svg, and video"


Interactive Content

Content that is geared towards end-user interaction. Includes "a, audio, button, embed, img, input, label, and any others"




These are different type of content that can be used to help create a document/webpage. It is sorted by different content models to allow for some examples to further look up.

Note: Headings should be used for proper structing of the content, and not the size of the text in each heading tag.
